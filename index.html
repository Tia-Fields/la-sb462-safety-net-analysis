Technical Alignment

Strengthening the Successor Agency Framework

The following technical safeguards are recommended to ensure the reorganized system remains operationally stable and strictly aligned with federal regulatory frameworks.

1.

Statutory Citation Modernization (R.S. 46:107(A)(1))

Analysis: The committee amendment retains an outdated reference to the "Food Stamp Act, Public Law 91-671".

Recommendation: Updating the citation to the Food and Nutrition Act of 2008, 7 U.S.C. § 2011 et seq. reduces legal ambiguity and aligns Louisiana’s reorganized code with current federal SNAP authorities.

Proposed Redline

"...as prescribed under Title I, IV-A, X, XIV, XVI, XIX, or XX of the Social Security Act and under the Food Stamp Act, Public Law 91-671 Food and Nutrition Act of 2008, 7 U.S.C. 2011 et seq."
2.

Fair Hearing and Continuation-of-Benefits Clarity

Analysis: While authority is redistributed, the code does not currently spell out how hearing rights and continuation-of-benefits protections will be anchored across multiple successor agencies.

Recommendation: Explicitly referencing 7 C.F.R. § 273.15 ensures that federal SNAP rules regarding fair hearings remain intact during and after the transition period.

Proposed Clarifying Language

"...subject to federal fair hearing protections, including continuation of benefits where applicable, in accordance with 7 C.F.R. § 273.15 and related notice requirements under 7 C.F.R. § 273.13."
3.

Successor Agency Interoperability Mandate

Analysis: The redistribution of eligibility, enforcement, and clinical programs across agencies creates an operational risk if legal authority moves faster than technical data systems.

Recommendation: A coordination mandate ensures that notice, case management, and records-transfer procedures remain interoperable to prevent service fractures.

Recommended Implementation Language

"The successor agencies shall maintain interoperable case administration, notice, and referral procedures sufficient to preserve continuity of services and compliance with federal law."

Legislative Advisory

Operational Safeguards for Program Integrity

Goal: Implementation Success

To support the policy goal of increasing agency accountability and system efficiency, this analysis identifies technical vulnerabilities that can be proactively resolved through targeted amendments.

To ensure successful implementation, the state must address how agency redistribution can create potential data coordination gaps. Left unaddressed, these gaps trigger eligibility determination inconsistencies and procedural closures. Procedural closures occur when households lose assistance because of paperwork, timing, or processing failures rather than a true loss of eligibility.

In federal Quality Control (QC) audits, these inconsistencies can contribute to administrative errors identified in federal QC review—rather than fraud—which drive error rates above the 6% threshold and create unintended state fiscal liability. With Louisiana’s recent SNAP error rate already around 7.6%, even modest increases in administrative misclassification, notice failures, or cross-agency data delays could deepen exposure above the 6% federal threshold.

ABAWD Exemption Synchronization

Ensuring medical or veteran status captured by DHHS clinicians automatically synchronizes with Eligibility systems prevents the misclassification errors that drive audit-related sanctions.

Interagency Data Continuity

Mitigating delays in data transfer between DHHS, DOJ, and State Police reduces administrative exposure by ensuring case changes are processed within strict federal timeframes.

Notice & Hearing Stability

To protect program integrity, maintaining due-process consistency during agency transition periods reduces the state's exposure to litigation and administrative penalty findings.

Objective: Position SB 462 for Operational Excellence and Regulatory Alignment.

Structural Optimization

Redistributing Authority for Specialized Oversight

The committee amendment to SB 462 replaces the monolithic DCFS structure with a specialized multi-agency model. This reorganization is designed to leverage existing agency strengths:

Clinical Specialization: Consolidating child welfare under DHHS aligns social services with clinical health and Medicaid oversight.

Legal Enforcement: Centralizing child support within the DOJ utilizes established legal and collection infrastructure.

Safety Response: Assigning intake to LSP professionalizes the state’s initial investigative response to child abuse reports.

Strategic Implementation Outlook

SB 462 represents a pivotal opportunity to modernize Louisiana's safety net. By redistributing authority to specialized agencies, the state can improve service quality and investigator professionalism.

Targeted amendments ensure that this structural transition achieves its intended efficiency without triggering the administrative errors that drive federal fiscal exposure.

Administrative design decisions at the agency level directly influence the state’s SNAP error rate and corresponding fiscal exposure. Proper coordination is the difference between a successful reorganization and a multi-million dollar federal penalty.
