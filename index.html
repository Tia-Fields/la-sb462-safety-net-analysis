Recommended Technical Amendments

The following amendments are proposed to ensure the reorganized safety net structure maintains technical alignment with federal regulatory frameworks and due process standards.

I.

Statutory Citation Modernization (R.S. 46:107(A)(1))

Analysis: The current committee amendment text in R.S. 46:107(A)(1) still uses an outdated "Food Stamp Act, Public Law 91-671" reference.

Recommendation: Updating the citation to the Food and Nutrition Act of 2008, 7 U.S.C. § 2011 et seq. would reduce legal ambiguity and better align Louisiana’s statute with current federal SNAP authorities.

Proposed Redline (R.S. 46:107(A)(1))

"...as prescribed under Title I, IV-A, X, XIV, XVI, XIX, or XX of the Social Security Act and under the Food Stamp Act, Public Law 91-671 Food and Nutrition Act of 2008, 7 U.S.C. 2011 et seq."
II.

Procedural Fair Hearings & Benefit Continuation

Analysis: The current language does not expressly reference the continuation of benefits pending a hearing. SNAP fair-hearing rules under 7 CFR 273.15 provide this protection in qualifying cases.

Recommendation: This amendment would clarify state hearing language and reduce due process and implementation risk during the transition of hearing functions between agencies.

Proposed Redline (Hearing Language)

"...is not acted upon with reasonable promptness as defined by federal regulations... subject to continuation of benefits pending hearing outcome in accordance with 7 CFR 273.15."

Technical Overview

Safety Net Decoupling & Successor Mandates

Senate Bill 462 proposes the reorganization of the Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS), creating a decentralized model that shifts core safety net programs to Louisiana Works (LWC) and the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS).

Under this structure, enforcement and oversight move to three specific legal and investigative successor agencies: the Department of Justice (DOJ), Louisiana State Police (LSP), and the Inspector General. This analysis examines the operational challenges of coordinating data-dependent functions across separate administrative structures.

Technical Note

The reorganization would benefit from a formal interagency data-sharing MOU or comparable protocol to help ensure that enforcement activities do not inadvertently contribute to eligibility errors without proper coordination.

Program Integrity

System Interoperability & QC Risk

Structural siloing of eligibility systems across separate agencies creates inherent obstacles to maintaining federal Quality Control (QC) standards. System fragmentation is a known contributor to elevated error rates, as data discrepancies can trigger improper benefit determinations and increased fiscal exposure.

Impact Analysis: ABAWD & Mandatory Exemptions

Exemption Coordination & Error Rate Risk

FRA 2023 created or clarified ABAWD exceptions for veterans, individuals experiencing homelessness, and former foster youth. DCFS’s more centralized structure may allow for stronger coordination of exemption-related information.

REORG RISK: Under reorganization, fragmented systems increase the risk of misclassification. Failure to accurately reflect exemption status across agencies drives up the federal error rate, creating a higher likelihood of improper denial or termination, along with increased exposure to federal error rate penalties.

Sunset Logic & System Controls

The FRA 2023 age expansion sunsets in 2030. Reorganization planning should ensure Louisiana Works’ eligibility systems can adjust when the FRA sunset takes effect.

Without proper controls, the state risks applying outdated rules after expiration, potentially leading to increased QC errors and potential fiscal exposure.

Technical Risk Analysis

Fiscal Exposure

Indirect Cost Allocation

Distributing functions across LWC, DHHS, DOJ, and LSP complicates federal reimbursement claims for overhead. This may increase the risk of reduced administrative funding matches.

Continuity Risk

Cross-Agency Priority Conflict

Moving enforcement to DOJ and LSP risks creating silos where legal priorities override the stabilization goals of safety net programs, potentially impacting participant outcomes.

IT Systems Risk

System Interoperability

The reorganization should require interoperable, timely data exchange across agencies to reduce eligibility and notice errors and ensure compliance with modern federal standards.
