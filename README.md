# SB 462: Safety Net Reorganization & Federal Compliance Analysis (Louisiana)

Technical analysis of Louisiana Senate Bill 462 (2026), focused on SNAP compliance, cross-agency data risks, and fiscal exposure.

---

## Overview

SB 462 dissolves DCFS and redistributes safety net functions across multiple agencies, including Louisiana Works and the Department of Health and Human Services.

This reorganization introduces implementation risks tied to federal SNAP requirements, particularly in eligibility, verification, and due process.

---

## Key Findings

- **Statutory Alignment Gap**  
Outdated SNAP references should be updated to align with the Food and Nutrition Act of 2008.

- **Due Process Exposure**  
State hearing provisions do not expressly reference federal benefit-continuation standards (7 CFR § 273.15).

- **Cross-Agency Data Risk**  
Fragmented systems increase the risk of misclassification of federally exempt populations under ABAWD rules.

- **System Design Risk (FRA 2023)**  
Work requirement changes introduce new programming and verification dependencies.

- **Fiscal Exposure**  
Administrative errors may increase SNAP error rates and create state financial liability.

---

## Why This Matters

SNAP error rates are driven primarily by administrative error—not fraud—most often tied to failures in eligibility, verification, and required notice procedures.

By shifting these functions across multiple agencies, SB 462 increases the risk of error if systems and coordination are not aligned.

System design and interagency coordination will determine whether the state maintains compliance or incurs fiscal exposure.
